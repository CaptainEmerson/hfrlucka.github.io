My Industry Roles

I've worked in a variety of roles throughout my career.

At Carnegie Mellon University:

I've worked as an IT Assistant in the Institute for Software Research (ISR) since my first week as a freshman at CMU. While cut a little early by COVID-19, I had a wonderful time running diagnostics and initial troubleshooting tasks, investigating hardware and software issues, and assisting the amazing 120 ISR students, staff, and faculty with various technological needs.

I was also a CMU gym attendant for my first semester, which taught me the importance of having a welcoming and positive attitude.

At Deutsche Bank:

During the summer after my junior year, I built a simulator in Java for messages sent between international credit/debit tracking systems in the Spring environment. I also built and presented a sentiment analysis application focusing on public traded companies, their stocks, and media bias in Python using ML and NLP. While I was at DB, I actively pushed for increasing diversity by giving presentations on the subject and engaging in large discussions.

At Consolidated Edison, Inc.

I began working at ConEd, the primary energy distributor, in high school and continued into my freshman year of college. While there, I was an Engineering Aide that worked in three different areas: Public Engineering, Work Management, and QA Testing.
